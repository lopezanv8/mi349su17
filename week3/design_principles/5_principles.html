Principles of Design

Affordance

The website I chose to represent the principle of affordance is Pandora. I choose Pandora because, as shown in the image,it has characterisitcs that represent their intended functions. For example, the pause design, pauses the music playing, the double arrow design, skips to the next song, and the thumbs up or thumbs up represent whether the user likes or dislikes the song.

Mapping

The website I chose to represent the principle of mapping is Facebook. I choose Facebook because in the image you can view the relationship between the image and the controls of Like, Comment, and Share. If you hover over the like, you will see different emojis appear, each of these then represent a different button that include like, love, haha, wow, sad, and angry.

Chunking

The website I chose to represent the principle of chunking is Fox News. I choose Fox News because the website displays how much information Fox can display within a certain area. The information is all one chunk but its slighlty seperated so that the viewer can know the difference between each news article.

Rule of Thirds

The website I chose to represent the principle of rule of thirds is CNN. I choose CNN because as shown in the image, CNN site's has a composition that is divided into thirds. There are three different columns and then three rows that you can kinda notice by the images within the articles.

Modularity

The website I chose to represent the principle of modularity is Pinterest. I choose Pinterest because Pinterest divides their website into rows, the width of the rows are all the same, but the length of each component varies depending on the image and text of each component. There are small components and large components, allowing a combination of all sizes to create the custom design.
